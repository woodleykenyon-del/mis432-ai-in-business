AI in Business

This portfolio documents labs and a group project from MIS 432 at Western Washington University. The course focuses on building simplified AI systems for business decision-making — understanding not just how these tools work, but why they produce value.

Labs
Lab 1

Drug Repurposing Discovery System

A simplified EveryCure-style AI system that surfaces drug repurposing candidates by identifying shared biological targets between drugs and diseases.

View project →
Lab 2

The Netflix Artwork Problem & A/B Testing

Simulated an A/B experiment comparing two artwork versions. Applied statistical testing to produce a data-driven rollout recommendation.

View project →
Lab 3

Spotify Recommendation Systems & Wrapped

Explored how Spotify uses implicit signals, collaborative filtering, and cosine similarity to personalize discovery. Built a working in-browser recommendation demo powered by the same data that generates Wrapped.

View project →
Lab 4

Uber Demand Forecasting & Responsible AI

Built a demand forecasting workflow for Friday-night Uber trips near WWU. Translated the forecast into an operational driver and surge recommendation, then examined prediction-decision gaps, distribution shift, and algorithmic governance.

View project →
Lab 5

Waymo Strategy Analysis

Simulated the role of a Waymo strategy analyst evaluating perception datasets, world models, reinforcement learning reward design, data budget allocation, and launch-market selection for city five.

View project →
Lab 6

Airbnb AI Systems Analysis: New Host Evaluation in Valencia, Spain

Evaluated Airbnb's matching system, embedding-based positioning, Smart Pricing, model interpretability, GenAI listing content, and regulatory risk for a first-time host. Produced a final recommendation using a conservative hybrid approach.

View project →
Lab 7

Agentic AI Lab: Job Scout Agent for a Mobile Job Pipeline

Built an agentic AI workflow in n8n with three custom tools to rank job opportunities while traveling abroad. Tested safe failure, bad-fit detection, and governance guardrails.
